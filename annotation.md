# Human Annotation Guidelines for AI Debates

## Overview
This project evaluates the argumentation quality, reasoning capabilities, and value alignment of various Large Language Models (LLMs) engaged in multi-turn debates. We are comparing different classes of models, including reasoning vs. non-reasoning models and small vs. large models. 

As an annotator, your objective is to evaluate a sampled set of AI-generated arguments and provide human judgments regarding their stance and strength.

## Your Task
You have been assigned a specific sheet in the `human_annotation.xlsx` file. Each row in your sheet represents a single argument turn from a broader AI debate. 

For each row, read the provided context and the `argument_text`, then fill in your evaluations in the designated columns.

### Read-Only Context Columns
*   **debate_id**: Unique identifier for the debate session.
*   **value_pair**: The core ethical or strategic values at odds in the debate (e.g., Care/Protection vs. Truthfulness).
*   **model_pair**: The two LLMs participating in the debate.
*   **agent**: The specific AI persona making the argument.
*   **turn_number**: The stage of the debate (1 to 4).
*   **argument_text**: The actual text generated by the AI agent.
*   **LLM_type** & **LLM_response**: The model's baseline classification of its own argument strength and stance.

### Columns for You to Populate
Please provide your independent assessment for the following fields:

*   **human_type**: Rate the structural and logical strength of the argument. 
    *   *Options*: `Strong` or `Weak`.
*   **human_response**: Determine the argument's stance relative to the debate's prompt or counter-agent. 
    *   *Options*: `Agreed` or `Disagreed`.
*   **confidence**: Rate your confidence in your evaluation on a scale of 1 to 5.
    *   *1* = Very unsure, *5* = Completely confident.
*   **notes**: (Optional) Add any brief comments if an argument is particularly ambiguous, flawed, or outstanding.

## Guidelines for Quality Annotation
1.  **Be Objective**: Base your evaluation of `human_type` (Strong/Weak) purely on the logic, clarity, and relevance of the argument, regardless of your personal stance on the issue.
2.  **Context Matters**: Use the `value_pair` column to ground your understanding of what the agent is attempting to argue.
3.  **Independent Judgment**: Do not simply copy the `LLM_type` and `LLM_response`. We are explicitly looking for areas where human judgment differs from the LLM's baseline self-assessment.
4.  **Consistency**: Apply the same criteria for argument strength across different models and agents.

Thank you for contributing to this research!
